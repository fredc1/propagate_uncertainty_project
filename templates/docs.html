Rules and Functions for Expression Input

The quick way of conveying the expression input rules is by saying that the expression must follow the compilation rules of python, but with the ^ character instead of ** for exponents. For mathematical expressions this is basically PEMDAS (parens, exponents, multiplication/division, addition/subtraction). Also, you may use only single character lower or upper case variables in your expression. One thing to note is that operators like ^ will consider the next character to be their only operand unless you use parens. List of operators: {{operators}}.
